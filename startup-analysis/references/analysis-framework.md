# Startup Analysis Framework

Use this framework to structure startup analysis. Keep each claim tied to evidence, a stated assumption, or an explicit open question.

## 1. Project Snapshot

- Name and one-liner
- Target customer
- Primary use case
- Geography and market scope
- Stage: idea, prototype, launched, revenue, scaling
- Main decision the user needs to make

## 2. Customer and Problem

Evaluate:

- Pain frequency: how often the problem happens.
- Pain intensity: how costly or annoying it is.
- Budget owner: who pays and why.
- Existing alternatives: manual work, incumbents, spreadsheets, agencies, in-house tools.
- Decision chain: user, buyer, approver, influencer.

Strong problems are frequent, expensive, urgent, and already have imperfect spending.

## 3. Market

Assess:

- Market category and subcategory.
- TAM, SAM, SOM when evidence allows.
- Growth drivers and timing.
- Regulation, platform dependency, and macro sensitivity.
- Profit pool and willingness to pay.

Avoid fake precision. Use ranges and mark assumptions.

## 4. Solution

Assess:

- Core value proposition.
- Workflow integration and switching cost.
- Technical feasibility.
- Time-to-value.
- Defensibility from data, distribution, network effects, workflow lock-in, or brand.

## 5. Competition

Map competitors as:

- Direct competitors solving the same problem.
- Indirect substitutes solving the same job differently.
- Status quo options.
- Potential entrants with distribution or data advantages.

Compare by customer segment, pricing, distribution, product depth, trust, switching cost, and wedge.

## 6. Business Model

Evaluate:

- Revenue model: subscription, transaction, usage, marketplace take rate, service, ads, licensing.
- Pricing logic and value metric.
- Gross margin drivers.
- Sales motion: self-serve, inside sales, enterprise, channel, community-led.
- Payback period and cash conversion.

## 7. Growth Strategy

Assess:

- Initial wedge and beachhead.
- Acquisition channels.
- Activation moment.
- Retention mechanism.
- Expansion path.
- Referral or network loop.

The best growth strategy should match the buyer, price point, urgency, and product complexity.

## 8. Financial Assumptions

State assumptions for:

- Users or accounts.
- Paid conversion.
- ARPU, AOV, or usage pricing.
- Monthly growth.
- Churn or retention.
- CAC and payback.
- Fixed and variable costs.

Use the financial script for a transparent baseline scenario.

## 9. Risks

Group risks by:

- Market risk
- Product risk
- Distribution risk
- Competitive risk
- Financial risk
- Team and execution risk
- Legal, regulatory, or compliance risk

For each major risk, propose a validation test.

## 10. Judgment

End with:

- Opportunity rating: high, medium, low, or uncertain.
- Key reasons.
- Biggest unknowns.
- Recommended next actions.
- Metrics to validate in the next 30, 60, and 90 days.
